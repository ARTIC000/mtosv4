The Monthly Touch Meeting Operating System

How we turn a recurring reporting call into a strategic, retention-building business conversation — every client, every month, with minimal prep and maximum consistency.

The one job of this manual

Make every client feel the value we deliver each month — informed, involved, and confident — while the account manager walks in fully prepared in under ten minutes.

01Why the Monthly Touch Matters

Clients rarely churn over a single bad metric. They churn when the value becomes invisible, the meetings feel repetitive, or a problem surfaces they feel should have been flagged sooner.

Why clients leave

  • Invisible value — work happens, but they can't feel it. A report is not perceived value.
  • Repetition — every call feels identical; no forward motion.
  • Surprise problems — an issue appears that "should have been caught."
  • No relationship — a vendor, not a partner they'd defend internally.

Why clients stay

  • Proactive transparency — we name issues first, with a plan attached.
  • Goal-tied framing — metrics linked to their business outcome.
  • Emotional reassurance — they renew on how supported they feel.
  • Strategic partnership — we think ahead, not just report back.

02The Five Outcomes Every Meeting Must Deliver

If a meeting doesn't produce all five, it wasn't a Monthly Touch — it was a status update.

  • Informed — the client knows exactly what we did, what worked, and what didn't.
  • Involved — they participated in the strategy; they didn't just receive a report.
  • Confident — perceived value increased, not decreased.
  • Supported — they know what's next and who owns it.
  • Captured — we recorded sentiment, churn signals, upsell openings, and content.

Each cycle runs the same four phases. The OS compresses prep and recap; the human owns the meeting and the relationship.

05The Wins & Issues Engine

Every meeting opens with specific wins and surfaces transparent issues. Wins reinforce value; issues build trust by demonstrating proactive leadership. We generate as many as are meaningful and backed by real data — never padding, never spin.

Three wins to lead with

Concrete, client-friendly, and tied to a business goal — not just a metric.

"Your Google Business calls jumped 28% to 342 — that's real patients reaching out directly."
"We improved rankings for 14 local search terms in your service area."

Two issues, handled transparently

Name it, state what's already underway, and give the next step. Calm, not alarming.

"Lead quality dipped slightly this month — we're already tightening targeting to lift conversion."
"Map rankings fluctuated in two areas; re-optimization is underway this week."

07The Content & Testimonial Capture System

Every positive meeting is also a chance to gather social proof — naturally, never scripted. The OS flags the opportunity; the account manager makes the ask.

When to ask (auto-detected)

The OS recommends an ask when sentiment is positive, recent wins exist, and the client has expressed satisfaction. If they've given a testimonial before, ask for a fresh update instead — a growth story, an ROI figure, a new win.

How to ask — low pressure

"If you'd be open to it, I'd love to capture a quick 30-second video on your experience so far — it really helps other business owners understand the impact of the work."

Questions that generate usable content

  • What changes have you noticed in your business over the last month?
  • Are customers mentioning they found you online more often?
  • What's made the biggest difference for your business recently?
The handoff

Mark the capture in the OS → it tags the meeting, creates a task, notifies Luisa in Marketing, and attaches the transcript moment for editing. Nothing slips through.

08Phases 3 & 4 — Recap & Follow-Through

Close the loop within 24 hours, every time. The OS drafts; the human approves.

  • Draft recap — paste notes or the Google Meet transcript; the OS drafts the client email and extracts action items.
  • Review & send — human-check, personalize, send within 24h. The SLA is tracked on the Follow-Up board.
  • Push tasks — action items sync to ClickUp with owners and due dates.
  • Route content — any flagged testimonial or quote auto-notifies Luisa with the clip.
  • Update CRM — sentiment and health score written back to GoHighLevel; next touch scheduled before the call ends.

09The Trusted-Advisor Layer

Account managers are not status messengers. The strategic recommendation is what makes a client feel guided rather than serviced.

"Here's what I'd recommend next…"  ·  "Here's what we're seeing across similar accounts…"

Look for upsell, cross-sell, retention, and CRO openings in every account. Position each as a move toward their goal, with the value and the first step attached — the same framing the OS surfaces on the Opportunities board.

10Mandatory Talking-Points Checklist

Non-negotiables. Every single meeting covers all ten.

  • Three wins, each tied to a business goal
  • Two transparent issues plus the plan underway
  • Campaign progress vs. last month's commitments
  • At least one strategic recommendation
  • Next priorities and timeline
  • Explicit client feedback solicited
  • Expectations realigned for next month
  • Testimonial / content opportunity assessed
  • Action items with owners + dates, confirmed aloud
  • Next touch scheduled before hanging up

Minimum viable stack

The OS + Google Workspace OAuth + ClickUp + GoHighLevel + Map Check covers prep, brief, recap, follow-up, and content routing end-to-end. Everything else is optional.

Map Check — the local-ranking engine

Our custom rank tracker is central to the prep brief. To connect it, the OS needs a read-only REST endpoint for scans, grid points, and keyword/location history; a scoped API token (per-tenant); and an optional webhook that fires when a monthly scan completes to trigger analysis. REST is sufficient; no database access required. That unlocks automatic month-over-month grid deltas summarized by service area, fed straight into the meeting brief as a win or a flagged issue.

The principle behind all of it

The OS prepares; the human decides. Automation removes the prep grind and the forgotten follow-up so the account manager can spend the meeting doing the one thing software can't — making the client feel guided, supported, and confident in the partnership.
